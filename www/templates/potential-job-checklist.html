If so, when?

Another hard part for babysitters is telling parents what they won’t do. Although you really want the job, there may be times when you have never done those things or you are not able to do them safely while looking after the kids. After all, the safety of the kids is your first responsibility and the parents will appreciate your honesty.

You must also be aware of things you ARE NOT comfortable with before you meet such as

Do you know these people?

Taking the kids to the park

Housework

Taking them swimming

Changing diapers

Lifting the kid if they are physically disabled

Using emergency tools such as an Epipen

Staying past a certain time

Having people you don’t know come over while you are there with the kids

Dogs, cats or other pets

Things to ask yourself during the pre-meeting

Would you feel comfortable in their home?

Would you feel comfortable with these kids?

Are you confident in what the parents expect from you?

Are you able to work when they need you?

Are you able to work longer if they are delayed getting back?

Is the pay they are offering acceptable?

IF YOU ARE COMFORTABLE with everything, you may agree to take the job. If you are underage, you may need your parents to approve the job first in the event they have some concerns.

IF YOU ARE NOT COMFORTABLE with something then let them know RIGHT AWAY. If you don’t want to say anything to the parents at this point, tell them that you have to speak with your parents or that you will have to check your schedule first. DO NOT take the job if you are not comfortable.
